OVERVIEW

Context

I have a chinchilla named Paris. He is the love of my life and centre of attention in my household. But it becomes an issue when the family is away from the house for more than one day.

I remember the first time my husband and I had to leave Paris at home for one week to visit Puerto Rico. It took us almost 5 days to find a place for Paris to stay when we were away. First, I explored some pet hotels online, and only one accepted small pets. However, that one was too far from my home, I was not able to drop Paris to that place. Fortunately, one of my neighbors saw my post on Instagram and offered to take care of Paris.

Due to this experience, I started to think about pet boarding options.

ROLE

TIMELINE

Problem

Going on vacation is supposed to be a relaxing experience, but for pet owners, extended time away from home can sometimes spell anxiety.

Where do you leave your pet when you’re away? This is usually a tough question, especially if you can’t find someone you know who has the time or experience to care for your pet. On the other hand, there are too many house boarding options online. And many people prefer to board their dogs at reputable facility.

The possibilities are seemingly endless, and it can be overwhelming.

Design

Ideation

Up until now, I had a vague idea of how the app will function. The most needed features would be: Search, Explore, Book, and Contact. Each represents a different cycle in the users progress towards finding the perfect boarding place.

Mapping the basic flow of the app forced me to figure each step on the path the users will take throughout the solution.

Usability Test

Transferred paper sketches into my initial high fidelity prototypes, I recruited 5 users to test my design ideas.

I wanted to answer two questions:

1: Can pet owners easily search and book a place to for their pets to stay?

2: Can pet owners use the message feature to state their requests?

I gathered and ordered user feedback by the level of frequency of the following type of comments. I grouped the issues in four distinct categories.
