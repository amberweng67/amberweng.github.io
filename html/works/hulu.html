Final Design

Have a brief idea of the movie/show
  • From the listing page, users can easily have a brief idea of the entire show by overall Tomatometer and Audience Score.
  • Then they can tap into the show detail page to view Tomatometer and Audience Score of each season.
  • This gives some information for users who do want to browse around but also a shortcut to make a quick decision and let users know at a glance whether or not a movie is worth time.
Reviews from peers and professionals
  • Ratings are often accompanied by qualitative reviews, which further aid deeper considerations.
  • By taping the expand icon, users could take a look at the reviews from professionals and peers.
  • The critic reviews, from Top 100 newspapers, magazines, and major websites, provide most-trusted movie critics influence user decision.
  • Reviews from peers give user a good idea of what normal users think of it.
Casts and crews
  • This page shows the detailed information about actors, producers, and directors with photos.
  • What makes this feture so useful is its cross-referencing. When users open the page for an actor, you’ll see their most well-known roles and awards he reveived.

Next Steps

Next steps would include usability testing to validate the feature’s success, to see if users feel higher rates of confidence and satisfaction in their decisions.

To stay focused on the decision making problems other than expanding the scope into another project, we did this design first and keep “stop suggesting” feature. Maybe there is much room to elevate “stop suggesting” feature since from our research the target users mainly focused on browsing homepage recommendations - which leads into the topic of personalization. By gaining more users’ inputs, like the reason of users’ intention of tapping “stop suggesting”, Hulu may provide better personalized recommended content to help user make confident desicions.
